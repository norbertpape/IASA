01.07.22

M.Sc. thesis defense at the KU Leuven on presheaf models of parametric dependent type theory

Working with the Belgian composer Simon Halsberghe to gain a better understanding of the Jacki Liebezeit Set. The Jacki Liebezeit set $J$ is the union of its subsets $J_n$, where each $J_n$ is the set of all possible rhythmical patterns consisting of 1s and 2s of length $n$, up to cyclic permutation:

$J := \bigcup_{n \in \mathbb{N}} J_n$

$ J_n := \left\{ a_0a_1\dots a_{k} \mid a_j \in \{ 1, 2 \}, k \in \mathbb{N} \text{ and } \sum_{i=0}^k a_i = n \right\}_{/\sim}$

where

$ a_0a_1\dots a_k \sim b_0b_1 \dots b_k \iff \exists l \in \mathbb{N} \forall j \in \{0,1, \dots, k\}: a_{(j + l)\mod (k+1)} = b_j$

Empirically, we have found the following progression and are still looking for a direct way of computing the cardinality of each subset $J_n$. Please contact us if you recognize the set and know more about it!
