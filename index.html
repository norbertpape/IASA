17.12.2022 First public showing of the results of the Beyond Gravity residencies. Luckily, many people of different ages tried the work, giving us valuable feedback. Further, we had super interesting public talks with Vivien Buchhorn, Christian Möller and Dominik Mohs, all the other participating artists and of course, and last but not least, the audience. We gave the work in progress the following title:

Digital - on touching digital objects

Digitus (lat. "Fingerbreite", "Zahl") stammt aus dem Proto-Indoeuropäischen *deyǵ- ("zeigen, weisen"). Virtual Realiy ermöglicht es uns, digitale Objekte im wahrsten Sinne des Wortes zu handhaben. Dieses Kurzschließen von Berührung und Abstraktion bietet einen fruchtbaren Rahmen für die Erforschung der Materialität immaterieller Objekte.

05.12.22 New rendering tools enable scenes containing pointclouds with a total of nearly 10 million points for an Oculus Quest standalone presentation. Further, we developed new modalities of interaction with the pointclouds, as well as differentiated render effects. In the demo video, for example, the back side of the objects is occluded.

26.11.22 We (Simon Speiser and Norbert Pape) are happy to have succeeded in developing a very intuitive mode of interaction with pointclouds, along with a tool that allows us to install objects in space. The demo video above is a few weeks old. By now we have developped fruther effects and managed to optimize our point cloud renderer such that it can handle up to nearly 10 million points!!! The renderer is built on the fantastic point cloud importer / renderer by Keijiro Takahashi. A new demo video will be posted soon.

Impact of O

The impact of O did not go unnoticed:

"On 29 June this year, Earth racked up an unusual record: its shortest day since the 1960s, when scientists began measuring the planet’s rotation with high-precision atomic clocks. Broadly speaking, Earth completes one full turn on its axis every 24 hours. That single spin marks out a day and drives the cycle of sunrise and sunset that has shaped patterns of life for billions of years. But the curtains fell early on 29 June, with midnight arriving 1.59 milliseconds sooner than expected." (The Guardian, 01.08.2022)

Norbert Pape

Norbert Pape is a dancer, choreographer, mathematician and pedagogue based in Berlin. He collaborated and toured internationally with artists such as Mette Ingvartsen, Antje Pfundtner, Christine Cuipke, Noé Soulier, Richard Siegal, William Forsythe, Nancy Stark Smith, Jan Ritsema, Bojana Cvejic, Dieter Heitkamp. His work has been presented in museums and venues such as Hamburger Bahnhof Berlin (2021), Mousonturm Frankfurt, Frankfurt LAB, Schwankhalle Bremen, PACT Zollverein, Festspielhaus Sankt Poelten, Theater Altenburg-Gera, Elizabeth-Kirche Berlin.

He studied classical and contemporary dance at the Hochschule für Musik und Darstellende Kunst Frankfurt am Main, obtained an M.A. in Choreography and Performance at the Institute for Applied Theater Sciences in Gießen under the direction of Prof. Bojana Kunst. Further, he recently obtained a B.Sc. (FU Berlin) and M.Sc. (KU Leuven) in Mathematics with research focus on Category Theory and Type Theory.

As a co-founder of ID Frankfurt and active member of the Performing Arts Forum, he has been committed to matters of self-organisation and creation of infrastructure that makes space for experimental and critical modes of production, exchange and research.

Fall 2021, he co-founded the International Agency for Space Affairs (IASA) together with Pêdra Costa, Göksu Kunak, Yuri Shimaoka and Elena Polzer.
